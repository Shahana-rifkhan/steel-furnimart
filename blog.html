How We’re Modernising Steel Furniture in 2025

By Team Steel Furnimart • 5 min read • Factory Upgrade

From CNC tube-cutting to powder-coat ovens with heat recovery, here’s how we improved precision, finish quality, and lead times.

In Q3 2025, we completed a phased upgrade to our Kerawalapitiya facility:

  • CNC tube/laser cutting: tighter tolerances & faster jig changes.
  • Robust fixturing: repeatable weld geometry and straighter frames.
  • Powder coat 2.0: low-VOC, better edge coverage, and improved salt-spray resistance.
  • In-line QA: digital gauges + traceable batch tags for each frame.

Outcome: up to 28% shorter lead times, fewer touch-ups, and a more consistent finish—especially on high-touch products like filing cabinets and wardrobes. These changes also reduce energy consumption per unit through heat recycling and smarter cure cycles.

What this means for customers

Cleaner edges, uniform colour across batches, and more reliable delivery dates. For institutional orders, we can now scale without sacrificing finish quality or structural performance.

Sustainability That Outlasts Trends

By Operations • 4 min read • ESG

We’ve introduced low-VOC powders, scrap loops, and energy-smart curing. Here’s how sustainability improves durability and cost.

Powder coating: Our latest polyester formulations lower VOCs and increase UV stability. Combined with a controlled cure window, we achieve better edge film build and fewer micro-chips in day-to-day use.

Closed-loop scrap: Off-cuts and test coupons are segregated by alloy and sent to approved recyclers, then bought back as certified feedstock where viable—reducing primary steel demand.

Energy: Heat-recovery on ovens and smart fans cut energy per unit by ~14% in pilot lines.

Result: furniture that resists corrosion longer, needs fewer touch-ups, and costs less to maintain over its lifetime.

Care & Maintenance: Keep Steel Looking New

By After-Sales • 3 min read • Maintenance

Weekly wipe-downs, monthly hinge checks, and how to treat scuffs on powder-coated surfaces—your easy upkeep plan.

  • Use pH-neutral cleaners; avoid abrasives that cut the powder film.
  • Touch-up pens for small chips; seal promptly to block moisture ingress.
  • Quarterly: check glide feet and tighten handle/lock hardware.

With basic care, our powder-coated finishes maintain gloss and colour uniformity for years—even in coastal humidity.
